# Stakeholder Updates


## Notification Matrix

### Severity 1 (Critical)
Notify immediately:
- Executive leadership (CEO/C-level)
- VP Engineering
- VP Product
- VP Customer Success
- Affected enterprise account owners
- PR / Communications team


### Severity 2 (High)
Notify:
- Engineering leadership
- Product leadership
- Customer Success lead
- Affected account managers


### Severity 3 (Medium)
Notify:
- Team lead of affected system
- Product manager (if feature-related)
- Account managers (if customer-facing)


### Severity 4 (Low)
- No proactive notifications required
- Track in backlog / ticketing system


## Standard Incident Update

Use this format for ALL stakeholder communications:


**Subject:** [Service Name] Incident Update #[N]

**Severity:** [SEV 1–4]  
**Status:** Investigating / Identified / Mitigating / Monitoring / Resolved  
**Time Detected:** [UTC timestamp]  


### Summary

[1–2 sentences in plain language describing what is happening]


### Customer Impact

- Affected services: [list]
- User impact: [who/what is affected]
- Scope: [all users / subset / specific accounts]


### Current Status

- What we know: [brief factual update]
- What we are doing: [current mitigation steps]
- Current blocker (if any): [what is slowing resolution]


### ETA

[Only include if reasonably confident; otherwise say “working to determine”]


### Prevention (if applicable)

[One line on what is being done to prevent recurrence or escalation]


## Messaging Guidance by Audience

### Executives
Focus on:
- Business impact
- Customer exposure
- Resolution timeline
- Risk containment

Avoid:
- Deep technical detail


### Engineering / Technical Teams
Focus on:
- Root cause progress
- System behavior
- Metrics, logs, signals
- Mitigation strategy


### Customer Success / Support
Focus on:
- Simple explanation
- Customer-facing wording
- Workarounds (if any)
- Expected resolution time


## Communication Cadence

- SEV 1: Every 30–60 minutes
- SEV 2: Every 1–2 hours
- SEV 3: As major updates occur
- SEV 4: No live updates required


## Post-Incident Communication

After resolution:

1. **Resolution notice** (within 1 hour)
2. **Preliminary summary** (within 24 hours)
3. **Full post-incident review link** (within 3–5 business days)


## Notes

- Keep updates factual and time-stamped
- Avoid speculation
- If unknown, explicitly state “investigating”
- Never over-promise ETAs
